# Spendly Go-To-Market Plan

Spendly’s exact target user is an engineering manager or team lead at a Series A or earlier startup with roughly 5 to 25 developers. This person usually owns day-to-day tooling decisions, can approve or strongly influence software spend, and sees the monthly AI tools charge on the company card but has never run a formal audit. They are not a CFO; they are technical, practical, and cost-conscious. They care less about abstract “AI strategy” and more about whether they are paying twice for overlapping capability.

Before wanting Spendly, this user is typically already searching or browsing around the problem. Common intent includes queries like “is Cursor worth it for my team,” “ChatGPT vs Claude for engineering teams,” and “reduce AI tool costs startup.” In parallel, they passively consume proof points in communities where people discuss tool ROI in public, such as Hacker News Show HN threads and candid operator discussions.

Primary distribution channels are where these users already spend attention:
- Hacker News
- r/ExperiencedDevs
- r/engineering
- Indie Hackers
- Lenny’s Newsletter Slack
- software engineering Twitter

The first 100 users in 30 days can be acquired with zero ad budget by executing one high-intent playbook repeatedly:
1. Publish a Show HN post: **“Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor for engineering teams.”**
2. Post focused threads in `r/ExperiencedDevs` and `r/SideProject` with concrete savings examples.
3. Send personalized LinkedIn DMs to 20 CTOs from YC W24 and W25 startups, referencing their likely AI stack and offering a free audit link.
4. Share in the Lenny’s Newsletter Slack tools channel with one short case-style walkthrough.
5. Recruit one indie hacker blogger to write about Spendly by offering a data-backed case study from anonymized audit patterns.

The unfair distribution channel is Credex. Credex already has relationships with companies actively buying AI credits, so this audience is pre-qualified: they already care about AI spend and savings execution. Spendly can become a native step in Credex onboarding: run audit first, identify waste, then route high-savings teams into discounted credit purchasing.

Week one traction targets, if execution works:
- **500 audit completions**
- **80 email captures**
- **3 inbound Credex consultation requests**

This GTM is intentionally narrow and operator-focused. The objective is not broad awareness in week one; it is to prove that a specific technical buyer (engineering manager/team lead) can self-serve into a credible savings report, share it internally, and become a Credex-qualified lead. Once that loop works, distribution can expand through SEO and partner integrations, but the first milestone is trust + conversion in one tightly defined segment.
