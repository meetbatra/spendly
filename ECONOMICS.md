# Spendly Unit Economics

Spendly’s economics are anchored to downstream Credex revenue, not to charging for the audit itself. A converted Credex lead is estimated at **$2,000 to $5,000 lifetime value (LTV)** based on typical startup AI credit usage.

## LTV Math (Explicit)

Assumption range per converted customer:
- Team buys discounted AI credits through Credex at roughly **$200 to $500 per month**.
- Retention is **12 to 24 months**.

So LTV range:
- Low case: `$200 x 12 = $2,400` (rounded into the stated conservative floor of ~$2,000).
- Mid/high case: `$500 x 12 = $6,000`.
- Extended case: `$500 x 24 = $12,000`.

Given early-stage uncertainty and churn risk, using an operational planning band of **$2,000 to $5,000** per converted lead is reasonable and conservative.

## CAC by Channel

1. **Show HN**
- Cash CAC is effectively `$0`.
- Real cost is founder effort (writing post, comments, follow-up, iteration on feedback).
- Best for fast trust-building and early distribution bursts.

2. **LinkedIn cold outreach**
- Cash CAC is `$0`.
- Time cost: about **2 hours per 20 DMs**.
- If response rate is **10%**, that is ~2 responses per batch.
- Economic question becomes time-to-qualified-call, not ad spend.

3. **Credex existing customer base**
- Cash CAC is `$0` because relationship already exists.
- This is the highest-leverage channel due to pre-qualified intent around AI spend.

## Funnel Math (Monthly)

Assumed top-of-funnel:
- **1,000 audit completions / month**
- **15% email capture** → `1,000 x 0.15 = 150 leads`
- **10% consultation booking rate from leads** → `150 x 0.10 = 15 consultations`
- **30% consultation-to-customer conversion** → `15 x 0.30 = 4.5 customers`

Outcome: about **4 to 5 new customers/month**.

Revenue translation at $2,000 LTV:
- `4 customers x $2,000 = $8,000`
- `5 customers x $2,000 = $10,000`

So expected monthly value creation from Spendly at this funnel level is **$8,000 to $10,000** in LTV-based revenue contribution.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months (Back-of-Envelope)

Target customer stock needed:
- `500 customers x $2,000 LTV = $1,000,000`
- or `200 customers x $5,000 LTV = $1,000,000`

Consultation requirement using 30% close rate:
- For 500 customers: `500 / 0.30 = 1,667 consultations`
- For 200 customers: `200 / 0.30 = 667 consultations`

Using the prompt’s aggressive planning frame:
- roughly **3,000 consultations** would require
- **30,000 email captures** at 10% consult rate, which requires
- **200,000 audit completions** over 18 months at a 15% capture rate.

This is difficult but plausible only if one breakout distribution event (viral Show HN or high-performing tweet thread) creates initial demand and referral/word-of-mouth compounds. The strategic takeaway: Spendly must maximize credibility of recommendations and report shareability, because those are the multipliers that make top-of-funnel economics work without paid acquisition.
